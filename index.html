Game Reviews

Rogue Galaxy

Level 5 have a history of creating distinctive titles that all have their own sense of identity whilst being very obviously a Level 5 game, this is partly due to the amount of polish that the graphics are given with a distinct cel shaded 2.5D look, an astounding soundtrack and well thought out gameplay mechanics it's hard to not love their work. Rogue Galaxy has found it's way to modern consoles as part of Sony's PS2 to PS4 promotion back in 2016 and I've only really gotten around to playing it recently! but so far, so good. The combat is fun even if I do generally prefer a slower paced JRPG instead of action RPG's it manages to capture the kind of charm that is often found within the Tales of series of games.

As the game progresses you can venure across the galaxy in your own flying pirate ship....how cool is that? There are ton's of side missions and weapons to collect/forge and this all culminates in an experience that will last most players roughly 60 hours of game time but you can probably eek out another 20-40 hours depending on what kind of gamer you are.

Battle Garegga

Another clasic has been reincarnated and risen like the pheonix but with new, improved and shinier plummage! Battle Garegga has always been a legendery title that has filled those in the know with anxiety when they attempt to navigate it's harsh terrain but M2 have done a fine job of opening up the mysteries of this title and laying them out in plain site for players both old and new, this is thanks to the help of M2 gadgets which display a medley of important information over the sides of the screen whilst still preserving the arcade perfect dimensions of the screen.
